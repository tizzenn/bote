Bote manages the shared accounts of a group of people: create one card per
event, note who paid for what and how it is split, and get the final report
with the minimum set of transfers needed to settle up.

Features:

* Event cards with avatar, date, location and attachments (photos and PDFs,
  local only).
* Attendees added from your contacts or by hand, each with a colored
  initials avatar, plus a configurable name for yourself.
* Entries (a good or service paid by one person) with an icon category,
  budgeted and spent amounts, and a receipt photo.
* Split by faders: equal parts or custom percentages, with pins to lock a
  share so moving one fader redistributes only the open ones.
* Final report with who pays whom, category breakdown, per-user payment
  marks and direct actions from the reminder notification.
* Serverless sharing: an event travels as a JSON file or a QR code and
  merges entry by entry (last edit wins, payment marks never lost,
  deletions stick).
* Full backup: every event into a single file, restorable without
  duplicates.
* Optional payment detector: turns your bank's purchase notification into an
  entry and recognizes your outgoing settlement transfer. Everything is
  processed on-device.

Optional automatic cloud sync (manual and hourly in the background) uses a
user-provided Supabase / PostgREST server and is disabled by default; every
event defaults to local-only sharing. Server-backed events are shared with a
small invitation QR that always fits, and synced data can be end-to-end
encrypted with a group passphrase. The app is fully usable offline.
